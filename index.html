CASE STUDY OF RASHMIKA MANDANNA

The Rashmika Mandanna deepfake case became a significant incident showcasing the misuse of advanced AI technology. A deepfake video featuring her face superimposed on content originally uploaded by a British-Indian influencer went viral in late 2023. The creator, Eemani Naveen, aimed to increase the follower count on a Rashmika Mandanna fan page he managed. This tactic was initially successful, boosting followers from 90,000 to 108,000 in just two weeks. However, the video sparked public outrage over privacy violations and the misuse of AI. Following a complaint, the Delhi Police's cybercrime unit investigated the case and eventually arrested Naveen in January 2024. Legal action was taken under various sections of the Indian Penal Code and the IT Act for identity theft and privacy violation. This case highlighted the urgent need for better regulations, public awareness, and ethical AI practices to prevent such misuse.

INSIGHTS AND SOLUTIONS:

How It Was Used:

The deepfake was leveraged to create a realistic yet misleading portrayal of Rashmika, exploiting her celebrity status to gain attention online.

OVERCOMING THIS CHALLENGE:

Technology Solutions: Develop advanced AI tools to detect and flag deepfakes. Utilize blockchain to verify the authenticity of media.

Regulations: Enforce stricter legal penalties for creating and spreading deepfakes, as India's IT Act Section 66D provides up to three years imprisonment and a fine.

Awareness: Educate the public about the existence and risks of deepfakes. Promote media literacy to help individuals identify potential manipulations. Platform Responsibility: Encouragement social media platforms to employ better content moderation and algorithms to counter deepfake content.

People can be seriously harmed by non-consensual deepfake videos that use and manipulate their likenesses for offensive or harmful content. Serious consequences may result from this, such as diminished career prospects, public humiliation, or strained interpersonal ties. There are several malicious uses of this technology - in videos and audios - and have been used to contreversialise several public figures. With the use of AI softwares, like the tool midjourney, several generated pictures of large political figures have been made, like controversial pictures of Joe Biden and Trump. However, deepfake technology can also be used positively and has been used in the entertainment industry to improve the outcomes of their actor's performances. Some examples are: Martin Scorsese’s “The Irishman”: An example of using deepfake technology to de-age actors. Star Wars: Rogue One: AI was used to produce the deceased actor Peter Cushing’s character, Grand Moff Wilhuff Tarkin. Roadrunner: In this Anthony Bourdain biopic, audio deepfake tech (voice cloning) was used to synthesize his voice.
